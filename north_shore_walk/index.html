Chicago to Wisconsin on Foot

Follow Sheridan Road, the Green Bay Trail, the Robert McClory Bike Path, and the Kenosha County / Pike Bike Trails from Edgewater north to Kenosha's harbor. It's not one single trail — it's a chain of connecting paths that trace the old Chicago, North Shore & Milwaukee interurban railroad corridor along Lake Michigan.

The Route
Four Trails, One Corridor

Sheridan Road (sidewalks)

~8 mi · Rogers Park → Wilmette

Walk Sheridan Road itself through Evanston — wide sidewalks, Northwestern's campus, and the stunning Bahá'í Temple. The North Shore Channel Trail parallels part of this but doesn't yet connect to the Green Bay Trail (still in feasibility study).

Sidewalk

Green Bay Trail

~9 mi · Wilmette → Highland Park

A paved multi-use path running parallel to the Metra UP-N line through Kenilworth, Winnetka, Glencoe, and Highland Park. Affluent North Shore suburbs, restaurants, parks, and easy lake detours. Becomes the McClory at Lake Cook Road.

Paved

Robert McClory Bike Path

~26 mi · Highland Park → WI State Line

The backbone. Beautiful ravines at Fort Sheridan, charming Lake Forest and Lake Bluff, then a more industrial stretch through North Chicago and Waukegan before opening back up near Zion. The trail follows an abandoned railroad corridor. Surface transitions from asphalt to crushed limestone heading north.

Paved (south) Crushed limestone (north)

Kenosha County + Pike Bike Trails

~14 mi · State Line → Carthage College, Kenosha

Cross the state line via a bridge over Russell Road. The Kenosha County Bike Trail runs in two segments with a gap through central Kenosha — the Pike Bike Trail along the lakefront bridges them. Finish at Carthage College or downtown Kenosha's harbor.

Paved Some on-street gaps
Day 1 The North Shore
Rogers Park → Highland Park · ~18–20 mi

Start where the Lakefront Trail ends and walk Sheridan Road through Evanston's leafy streets and Northwestern's campus. Stop at the Bahá'í Temple in Wilmette, then pick up the Green Bay Trail through the affluent North Shore suburbs. End in Highland Park near Ravinia — plenty of food, Metra access, and lodging options.

Day 2 The McClory
Highland Park → Zion / Waukegan · ~18–20 mi

The backbone of the route: the Robert McClory Bike Path. Beautiful ravines at Fort Sheridan, charming Lake Forest and Lake Bluff, then a more industrial stretch through North Chicago and Waukegan before the trail opens up again near Zion and Illinois Beach State Park.

Beautiful bluffs and ravines overlooking Lake Michigan. Rated 4.8 ★. Worth a short detour down to the beach if the weather's nice. Bathrooms in the parking lot.

North Chicago / Waukegan
~1:30 PM · Miles 13–16
The trail runs behind old industrial buildings — less scenic but interesting in its own way. You can feel the old railroad corridor here. Fewer services and less shade than the North Shore sections, so top off your water before Lake Bluff.
🏖️
Illinois Beach State Park
~3:30 PM · Mile 18 · Zion
Beautiful Lake Michigan beach. The park has a campground right on the trail — if you're open to camping one night, this is a far cooler option than a Waukegan chain hotel. You'd fall asleep to the sound of the lake.

Day 3 Into Wisconsin
Zion / Winthrop Harbor → Kenosha · ~18–20 mi

Cross the state line, pick up the Kenosha County Bike Trail, then the Pike Bike Trail along Kenosha's scenic lakefront. Finish at Carthage College or downtown Kenosha's harbor. Celebrate with food and drinks at the lakefront, then figure out your ride home.

Logistics
Practical Notes
🚂 Getting Home

Metra UP-N runs to Kenosha on weekday limited service. Best bet: rideshare from Kenosha to Waukegan and catch a frequent Metra south. Or have someone pick you up in Kenosha.

🎒 Gear

Small pack with spare socks, blister kit, portable charger, and enough water for the exposed Waukegan-to-Zion stretch where services are sparse. Sunscreen for the shadeless northern sections.

⏰ Timing

Start early each day (7 AM). The North Chicago/Waukegan stretch on Day 2 should be walked in full daylight. Summer gives you the longest window.

🚻 Facilities

Abundant through the North Shore suburbs (cafés, libraries, Metra stations). Sparse between North Chicago and Zion — plan accordingly. Illinois Beach State Park has facilities.

🚂 The Metra Bailout

Unlike Lake Geneva or the Lakefront Path, this route runs parallel to the Metra UP-N line for most of its length. If you bonk on any given day, you're never more than a couple miles from a station that'll take you home (or to your hotel). Massive safety net.
