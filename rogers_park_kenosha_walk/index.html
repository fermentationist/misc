Chicago to Wisconsin on Foot

Follow Sheridan Road, the Green Bay Trail, the Robert McClory Bike Path, and the Kenosha County / Pike Bike Trails from Edgewater north to Kenosha's harbor. It's not one single trail — it's a chain of connecting paths that trace the old Chicago, North Shore & Milwaukee interurban railroad corridor along Lake Michigan.

The Route
Four Trails, One Corridor

Sheridan Road (sidewalks)

~8 mi · Rogers Park → Wilmette

Walk Sheridan Road itself through Evanston — wide sidewalks, Northwestern's campus, and the stunning Bahá'í Temple. The North Shore Channel Trail parallels part of this but doesn't yet connect to the Green Bay Trail.

Sidewalk

Green Bay Trail

~9 mi · Wilmette → Highland Park

A paved multi-use path running parallel to the Metra UP-N line through Kenilworth, Winnetka, Glencoe, and Highland Park. Affluent North Shore suburbs, restaurants, parks, and easy lake detours. Becomes the McClory at Lake Cook Road.

Paved

Robert McClory Bike Path

~26 mi · Highland Park → WI State Line

The backbone. Beautiful ravines at Fort Sheridan, charming Lake Forest and Lake Bluff, then a more industrial stretch through North Chicago and Waukegan before opening back up near Zion. Surface transitions from asphalt to crushed limestone heading north.

Paved (south) Crushed limestone (north)

Kenosha County + Pike Bike Trails

~14 mi · State Line → Carthage College, Kenosha

Cross the state line via a bridge over Russell Road. The Kenosha County Bike Trail runs in two segments with a gap through central Kenosha — the Pike Bike Trail along the lakefront bridges them. Finish at Carthage College or downtown Kenosha's harbor.

Paved Some on-street gaps

Logistics
Practical Notes
🚂 Getting Home

Metra UP-N runs to Kenosha on weekday limited service. Best bet: rideshare to Waukegan and catch a frequent Metra south. Or have someone pick you up in Kenosha.

🎒 Gear

Small pack with spare socks, blister kit, portable charger, and enough water for the exposed Waukegan-to-Zion stretch. Sunscreen for the shadeless northern sections.

⏰ Timing

Start early each day (6–7 AM). Summer gives you the longest daylight window. The 2-day plan requires dawn starts.

🚻 Facilities

Abundant through the North Shore suburbs (cafés, libraries, Metra stations). Sparser between North Chicago and Zion. Illinois Beach State Park has facilities.

🚂 The Metra Bailout

This route runs parallel to the Metra UP-N line for most of its length. If you bonk on any given day, you're never more than a couple miles from a station that'll take you home or to your hotel.
